# Trading Analysis — Mistakes & Learnings

## Rules for Chart Pattern Analysis

### 1. Golden Cross ≠ SMA50 > SMA200
- A golden cross is the **moment** SMA50 crosses above SMA200, not just being above it
- Always state **when** the crossover happened, not just that it exists
- If you can't determine the exact timing, say "SMA50 is above SMA200" — don't call it a golden cross

### 2. Distinguish Sharp Reversals from Consolidation Patterns
- A -3%+ single-day drop from a high on strong volume = **shooting star / reversal candle**
- A bull flag = gradual, low-volume drift down over multiple days
- Never call a sharp reversal a "bull flag" or "consolidation"

### 3. V-Shape vs Rounded Bottom vs Cup
- V-shape = sharp drop then sharp recovery (weeks, not months)
- Rounded bottom = gradual, curved recovery over months
- Cup & handle = needs prior high as the "rim" — don't use this unless price is near a prior peak
- A 3-6 month recovery from lows is a **rounded bottom**, not a V-shape

### 4. Don't Over-Lean Bullish
- When oscillators show sell and there's a sharp reversal candle, call it **neutral-bearish short term**
- "Healthy correction in an uptrend" is a cop-out when the data shows clear bearish short-term signals
- Separate long-term bias (weekly) from short-term bias (daily) explicitly

### 5. Price Action > Indicators
- Read the **price structure** first (highs, lows, candle shapes, volume)
- Then confirm with indicators (RSI, MAs, ADX)
- Don't reverse-engineer patterns from indicator numbers alone

### 6. Be Precise with Terminology
- "Confirmed" means verified with evidence — don't use loosely
- State the timeframe for every claim (daily SMA, weekly pattern, etc.)
- If data is ambiguous, say so — don't pick the most bullish interpretation
