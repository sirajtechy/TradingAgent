## 4 Entry Methods

### 1. Standard Breakout
Price breaks above a well-formed base or resistance on significantly higher-than-average volume. Cleanest, most common signal.

**Trigger:** Vol > 2× Avg → BUY

### 2. Pivot Breakout
Breakout from a specific pivot-level consolidation inside a larger base. More precise entry point, allows tighter stop.

**Trigger:** Pivot High Break + Volume

### 3. Shakeout Entry
A false breakdown below support that rapidly reverses upward, trapping short-sellers. High-conviction reversal signal.

**Trigger:** False Break → Snap Back

### 4. Pullback Entry
After a breakout, price retraces to MA10 or MA20. Lower-risk entry for those who missed the initial breakout.

**Trigger:** Retrace to MA10 / MA20

## Pattern Playbook

### Pattern 1: VCP (Volatility Contraction)
1. Strong advance on high volume
2. First consolidation (wider range)
3. Second consolidation (tighter range)
4. Third contraction (volume near zero)
5. Break above pivot on 2× vol → **BUY**

### Pattern 2: Sideways Base / Flat Base
1. Stock moves sideways 4-8 weeks
2. Volume contracts during consolidation
3. Tight 10-15% price range
4. Up days outperform down days in base
5. Breakout to new highs → **BUY**

### Pattern 3: Tight Flag / Pocket Pivot
1. Sharp advance (flagpole) on high volume
2. Shallow, tight pullback (flag body)
3. Volume dries up during flag
4. Max 50% retrace of flagpole
5. Break above flag top → **BUY**

## Stage Analysis: When to Act

### Stage 1 — Accumulation
**Below MA10/MA20.** Sideways or declining. Institutions quietly accumulating.

**Action:** 👀 Watch

### Stage 2 — Momentum Phase
**Breaks above MA10/MA20 with volume expansion.** The ONLY stage worth trading.

**Action:** ✅ TRADE

### Stage 3 — Exhaustion
**Extended run, climax moves, wide & loose price action.** Reduce or exit.

**Action:** ⚠️ Reduce

### Stage 4 — Decline
**Distribution complete. Below MA200.** Avoid entirely.

**Action:** ❌ AVOID
