## Chart Pattern Identification Rules

### Golden Cross / Death Cross
- Golden cross = the **moment** SMA50 crosses above SMA200 (not just being above)
- Death cross = the **moment** SMA50 crosses below SMA200
- If SMA50 is already well above SMA200, say "SMA50 is above SMA200" — the cross happened in the past
- Always try to estimate **when** the cross occurred based on performance data

### Reversal Patterns
- Shooting star: single large red candle from a high, upper wick, on strong volume
- Doji: indecision candle at top/bottom of trend
- Engulfing: current candle body fully covers prior candle body
- A -3%+ single-day drop from 52-week high on above-avg volume = reversal signal, NOT consolidation

### Continuation Patterns
- Bull flag: gradual, LOW-volume drift down over multiple days/weeks after a rally
- Ascending triangle: higher lows with flat resistance
- Pennant: converging trendlines after sharp move
- NEVER call a sharp single-day reversal a "bull flag"

### Bottom Patterns
- V-shape: sharp drop + sharp recovery (total duration: days to weeks)
- Rounded bottom: gradual curved recovery over months — U-shaped
- Cup & handle: needs prior high as the "rim" — price must approach that prior high
- Double bottom: two distinct lows at similar price, W-shape

### Top Patterns
- Double top: two peaks at similar price, M-shape
- Head & shoulders: three peaks, middle highest
- Rising wedge: higher highs + higher lows converging — bearish

## Indicator Interpretation Matrix

| Indicator | Bullish | Neutral | Bearish |
|---|---|---|---|
| RSI | 40-60 (healthy) | 50 | >70 overbought, <30 oversold |
| ADX | >25 (trending) | 20-25 | <20 (no trend) |
| MA Recommend | >0.5 strong buy | 0 to 0.5 | <0 sell |
| Oscillator Recommend | >0.2 | -0.2 to 0.2 | <-0.2 |
| Volume vs avg | Above avg on up days | Normal | Above avg on down days |

## Conflicting Signals Framework
When MAs say BUY but oscillators say SELL:
- This typically means **pullback within an uptrend**
- Short-term: neutral-to-bearish
- Long-term: still bullish
- Key action: watch the nearest MA support (SMA20 or SMA50)

When MAs say SELL but oscillators say BUY:
- This typically means **bounce within a downtrend**
- Short-term: bullish bounce
- Long-term: still bearish
- Key action: watch resistance at the declining MAs

## Multi-Timeframe Analysis Template
1. **Weekly first** — identify the dominant trend and major pattern
2. **Daily second** — identify the current phase within that trend
3. **State bias separately** for each timeframe
4. **Conflict is normal** — weekly bullish + daily bearish = "long-term uptrend with short-term pullback"

## Support/Resistance Hierarchy
1. All-time high / low (strongest)
2. 52-week high / low
3. SMA200
4. SMA50
5. SMA20 / EMA20
6. EMA10
7. VWAP (intraday)
8. Round numbers ($50, $100, etc.)

When multiple levels cluster within 2%, call it a **confluence zone** — these are strongest.
